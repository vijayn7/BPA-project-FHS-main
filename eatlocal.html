Why it benefits the environment?
Eating locally can help significantly reduce your carbon footprint. When growing and selling food, some types of food are better for the environment than others. In the U.S, shipping foods accounts for 13% of all carbon emissions. This staggering number is explained by how foods are transported. Food is taken by car, truck, and plane among other methods of transportation, and all of these require the burning of fuels, releasing carbon into the atmosphere. Choosing to purchase locally grown food can cut out a majority of these transportation emissions and help limit the amount of carbon you are responsible for.

How can taking shorter showers be beneficial for the average customer?
Eating locally can have benefits for customers too. Of the many benefits of eating locally, one of the biggest is being able to eat only fresh produce. Due to many farmers lacking industrial sized storage equipment, produce is picked and sold as soon as possible, and this ensures you get fresh produce. Along with this, you are supporting local economies.
